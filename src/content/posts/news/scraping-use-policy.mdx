The Fire Department regularly gets questions from people who want to scrape messages on the Slack, but don't know what's allowed.

After months of discussion with the community, we have a draft Scraping policy. It's not perfect, but it's a starting point, open to discussion and improvement.
This policy takes effect on Monday, June 15.

## Rules for Scraping on the Hack Club Slack

"Scraping" means collecting and storing message content for later retrieval, analysis, indexing, training or reuse. Processing live message events without storing message contents is not considered scraping.

If a user withdraws consent for messages that were collected through explicit consent, reasonable efforts should be made to remove those messages from future datasets, indexes and storage.

Any use of scraped messages remains subject to the Code of Conduct.

Projects that scrape messages should clearly disclose what data is being collected and how it is being used.

### What is allowed

1. Scraping your own messages.
2. Scraping message metadata that does not include message contents or sender (eg: reactions, timestamps or message count) for any messages sent on or after June 15 2026.
3. Scraping the contents and identity of other users' messages with their clear consent. Consent should be given through a written and reasonably verifiable record (eg: a Slack message or form submission)
4. <span id="what-is-allowed-4"></span>Scraping messages sent in a channel that
   you own and manage after notice has been publicly posted. Notice should be
   clearly visible through an anchored message or channel topic.
5. <span id="what-is-allowed-5"></span>Scraping messages in channels owned by
   another user when both: - Notice has been provided to channel members - The
   channel owner has consented to it
6. Scraping messages in informational channels specifically designated by the FD and/or HQ for announcements, event support, documentation or other public information purposes. Right now, these channels are:
   - <SlackChannel id="announcements" />
   - All official bulletin channels for Hack Club programs
   - <SlackChannel id="help" /> and dedicated help channels, like
     <SlackChannel id="stardance-help" /> and
     <SlackChannel id="identity-help" />
7. Processing messages that meet the above criteria for analysis, indexing, search, retrieval, summarisation, analytics or other similar purposes. If your use purpose is not mentioned here, reach out directly to the FD via Shroud to confirm.
8. Sending message content to any third party service for processing is permitted only when:
   - The messages meet the above criteria
   - The message authors have been clearly informed that message contents may be shared with external service providers and have the opportunity to easily opt out prior.
9. Training AI models on messages only when:
   - The messages meet the above criteria
   - The message authors have explicitly consented to their messages being used for model training.

### What is not allowed

1. Scraping messages in channels you do not own or manage without the required consent (see [What is allowed - 5](#what-is-allowed-5))
2. Scraping messages in channels you own or manage without first providing clear notice (see [What is allowed - 4](#what-is-allowed-4))
3. Scraping messages sent before June 15 2026 without explicit consent from every message author. The only exception is informational channels.
4. Making scraped message content publicly accessible on the open web. If scraped messages are made visible to people other than the person who collected the messages, access must be restricted to authenticated Hack Clubbers (such as via HCA) or another agreed on and approved audience.
5. Training AI models on messages without explicit consent from the message authors.
6. Using scraped messages to harass, impersonate, sexualise, profile or otherwise target others in ways that would violate the Code of Conduct.

Thank you for all the questions you've asked that have helped us think about this. Can't wait to hear from you and know what you think and what you'd like to see!
