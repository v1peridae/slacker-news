I get people asking me for project ideas. I also see a lot of projects that have already been built many times. Meanwhile, I am up to my elbows in projects I wish people would build. (I am working on a piece which lists some of them).

Thinking about this mismatch, I've decided that the market is oversaturated with solutions to general, obvious problems, but is quite undersaturated with solutions to specific problems.

Many of you know that I used to work in debate. One of my jobs there was to figure out who qualified for the New York Debate League championship. I needed to look at students' performance in every NYDL tournament across an entire school year, to find every individual student who went 3-1 or 4-0 in the varsity division at least once. This is harder than it sounds. The results are all public on [tabroom.com](https://www.tabroom.com), but in a format that is inconvenient and subtly wrong for my purposes. What I really needed was a tabroom API. I found two, but neither one appeared to work, at least for what I was doing.

This is just an example. But notice how *specific* it is.

Matt Bruening, a writer I enjoy, recently made an [AI-powered legal research tool](https://www.nlrbedge.com/p/ive-got-more-ai-labor-law-research). Its job is to query US labor law, which mostly consists of documents published by the National Labor Relations Board. AI spending has reached levels that made "we are in an AI bubble" a mainstream opinion. Yet somehow, nobody else thought to do what Bruenig did, and now he has 20 paid subscribers. Why? "Do more AI!" is general. "Use AI to query the NLRB's published documents" is specific.

Peter Thiel--who would not like Matt Bruenig, and vice-versa--wrote *Zero to One*, a famous book about entrepreneurship. He comments, "All happy companies are different: each one earns a monopoly by solving a unique problem. All failed companies are the same: they failed to escape competition."

If you want to make something inspired, something special and different, or just something that attracts paying customers, my advice to you is to go find people who are busy trying to get something specific done. Even better if they aren't technical. They will have unmet needs you can be the first to fill.
