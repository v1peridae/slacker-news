**What happened this week**

849 projects were approved across YSWS programs between May 1 and May 8. Of those. 769 passed cleanly, 49 were flagged for changes, and 21 were rejected. 58 fines were issued and 58 projects were eliminated.

**Coverage**

Of the 839 checked this week: 91.7% passed the automated check cleanly, 5.8% were flagged for changes, and 2.5% were rejected. 6.4% were reviwed in a second pass, and 9.5% of them were reviewed thoroughly in a third pass. This is where I check the repo,demo and hearbeats, and make sure the hours approved correlate with the project. I'm aiming to scale this part of the process to around 15% by the end of the month.

**Cumulative totals**

Since the spot check pipeline started: 22,133 total checks, 173 fines issued, 286 projects eliminated.

**Resolution**: 50 of its 77 projects were fined this week. 36 of them were flagged as Needs Changes on third pass and 3 were rejected, with 6 rejected on second pass. 

**BakeBuild** continues to have the same issue as last week: all 9 projects were flagged on first pass because the repo format didn't match what the automated check expected. All 9 passed on second review.

**HC: The Game**: 11 of its 64 projects were flagged on first pass. All 11 passed on second review as they were correctly justified.

**Hardware stuff**

@notaroomba is running experiments to detect cases of double-dipping on hardware projects, where the same physical build gets submitted to multiple YSWS programs. This is a gap in the current pipeline since the automated checks are optimized for software repos and don't handle hardware project they way they should be.

I'm also trying to figure out a better way to track hardware projects in general. If you have ideas on how to improve hardware project tracking and verification, please DM me at @lfd on Slack.
